ARTICLE 2 — DEFINITIONS

2.1 "Services" — all software deployment, configuration, integration, managed hosting, monitoring, maintenance and support activities described in Article 3 (Part A — Active Services), excluding any hardware procurement.

2.2 "Platform" — the qui3tly.cloud infrastructure cluster (servers Master and Lady) and all associated software, containers, networks and automation scripts.

2.3 "Branches" — the four Client locations: Budva Lazi bb (Retail Store), Budva Lazi bb (HQ/Office), Obilaznica pored Parme (Ceramics Store), and Lastva Grbaljska bb (Warehouse).

2.4 "Incident" — any unplanned interruption or degradation of a Service, classified by severity as Critical, High, Medium or Low per Article 7.

2.5 "Business Day" — Monday through Friday, 09:00–17:00 CET, excluding Montenegro public holidays.

2.6 "Effective Date" — January 1, 2026, being the date from which services commenced and from which billing applies, irrespective of the date of signing.

2.7 "Annex A" — the scope addendum listing future service phases (ERP, POS, Inventory, CCTV, Access Control, Time Evidence, Mobile Apps), to be delivered under separate milestone agreements appended to this Contract.

ARTICLE 3 — SCOPE OF SERVICES

3.1 Part A — Active Services (live from Effective Date). The Provider delivers and manages the following services, which are already operational:

3.1.1 Cloud Infrastructure — Managed Hosting

Deployment, configuration and ongoing managed hosting of the qui3tly.cloud Platform (servers Master and Lady). Includes: reverse proxy and HTTPS (Traefik); security stack (CrowdSec IPS/IDS, Authelia SSO, fail2ban); DNS and ad-blocking (Pi-hole/Unbound); monitoring and alerting (Prometheus, Grafana, Loki, Uptime Kuma); automated daily backups; container management (Docker/Portainer).

3.1.2 VPN Mesh Network (Headscale/WireGuard)

Deployment and management of a zero-trust VPN mesh network connecting all four Branches and the qui3tly.cloud Platform. Includes: WireGuard encryption (ChaCha20-Poly1305), full-mesh topology with automatic failover, MagicDNS hostname resolution, role-based node access control. Branch VPN endpoints: HQ 192.168.110.0/24 (Tailscale 100.64.0.4), Retail 192.168.111.0/24, Ceramics 192.168.112.0/24, Warehouse 192.168.113.0/24. Rollout to remaining Branches in progress per agreed schedule.

3.1.3 Email Infrastructure (@norma.me)

Deployment and management of a full Mailcow email stack for the domain @norma.me. Includes: Postfix SMTP, Dovecot IMAP/POP3, SOGo webmail, Rspamd spam filtering, ClamAV antivirus, TLS 1.3 enforced, SPF/DKIM/DMARC, unlimited mailboxes and aliases, 99.9%+ deliverability guarantee.

3.1.4 File Storage & Collaboration (Nextcloud + OnlyOffice)

Deployment and management of Nextcloud with OnlyOffice integration on the Lady server. Includes: unlimited file storage, desktop and mobile sync clients (Windows, macOS, iOS, Android), real-time collaborative document editing (OnlyOffice — Writer, Calc, Impress), version history and rollback, external sharing with password protection and expiry, calendar and contacts sync, GDPR-compliant data handling.

3.2 Part B — Future Service Phases (Annex A). The following service phases are agreed in principle and shall be delivered under separate milestone schedules appended as Annex A to this Contract. Each phase shall be activated by a signed Annex A amendment specifying scope, timeline and any additional fees:

Annex A — Future Delivery Scope (subject to signed milestone agreements):

PHASE 2: ERP System (Odoo) — business management, HR, payroll, document workflows
PHASE 2: Accounting & Finance — automated bookkeeping, invoicing, Montenegro tax exports
PHASE 2: Point of Sale (POS) — unified POS across retail branches, inventory sync
PHASE 2: Inventory Management — real-time multi-branch stock tracking, barcode scanning
PHASE 2: CRM — customer data, loyalty programme, order history
PHASE 3: Warehouse Management System (WMS) — Lastva Grbaljska operations, receiving, dispatch
PHASE 3: Fleet Tracking — vehicle and delivery management, route optimization
PHASE 3: CCTV Surveillance — Frigate NVR, AI detection, all 4 branches (hardware by Client)
PHASE 3: Access Control — RFID/biometric door management, audit logs (hardware by Client)
PHASE 3: Time & Attendance — employee clock-in/out, shift management, payroll integration
PHASE 3: Mobile Applications — Manager Dashboard, Warehouse, Sales Rep, Employee Self-Service, Customer Loyalty (iOS & Android)

3.3 Hardware Exclusion. This Contract covers software, configuration, integration, development and managed services only. The procurement, installation and maintenance of all physical hardware is exclusively the responsibility of the Client.

ARTICLE 9 — SECURITY OBLIGATIONS

9.2 Client Obligations. The Client shall: maintain physical security of hardware at all Branches; protect login credentials; promptly report any suspected security incident; ensure terminated employees' access is revoked within 24 hours.

ARTICLE 12 — FORCE MAJEURE

12.1 Neither Party shall be liable for delays caused by events beyond its reasonable control, including natural disasters, armed conflict, government action, nationwide internet infrastructure failure or prolonged power grid outage.

12.2 The affected Party shall notify the other within 48 hours of the force majeure event arising.

12.3 If the event persists for more than 30 calendar days, either Party may terminate without penalty by written notice.

ARTICLE 13 — LIABILITY LIMITATION

13.1 The Provider's total aggregate liability under this Contract shall not exceed three (3) times the monthly service fee (€3,000.00 net).

13.3 The limitations in 13.1 and 13.2 shall not apply in cases of wilful misconduct, gross negligence, or a confirmed GDPR violation.

ARTICLE 14 — TERM & TERMINATION

14.1 Effective Date & Initial Term. This Contract is effective from January 1, 2026 and has an initial term of three (3) months corresponding to the advance payment period, running through March 31, 2026.

14.2 Renewal. After the initial term, the Contract automatically continues on a month-to-month basis until terminated by either Party.

14.3 Termination by Notice. Either Party may terminate by giving 30 days' written notice.

14.4 Termination for Cause. Either Party may terminate immediately if the other Party commits a material breach that remains unremedied for 15 calendar days after written notice.

14.5 Effect of Termination. On termination: (a) the Provider shall deliver Client data per Article 8.5; (b) any unused portion of a pre-paid advance shall be refunded pro-rata within 30 days; (c) the Provider shall provide portability assistance per Article 10.4 for 30 days.

15.1 This Contract is governed by the laws of Montenegro, in particular the Law on Obligations (Zakon o obligacionim odnosima).
